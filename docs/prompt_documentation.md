# PromptForge: Prompt Documentation

This document serves as a structured repository and historical record of the AI prompts used during the development of **PromptForge**. It captures the engineering intent, the iterative refinement process, and the specific inputs that drive the platform's intelligent features.

## 1. Project Overview

*   **Project Name:** PromptForge
*   **Project Objective:** To provide a premium, collaborative platform for prompt engineers and AI developers to discover, create, version, and share high-performance AI prompts.
*   **Description:** PromptForge treats prompts as high-value code assets. It bridges the gap between creative prompt design and technical AI integration by providing structured workspaces, version control, and community-driven analytics.
*   **Role of AI Prompts:** In PromptForge, prompts are the core logic layer. They facilitate everything from content moderation and quality scoring to automated tagging and semantic discovery.

## 2. Prompt Engineering Strategy

*   **Why Prompts Were Used:** Prompts were utilized to handle complex, non-deterministic tasks like qualitative analysis, natural language understanding, and automated content generation that would be inefficient or impossible to implement with traditional hard-coded logic.
*   **AI Systems Used:**
    *   **GPT-4 / GPT-3.5:** Core reasoning and prompt execution.
    *   **Claude:** Utilized for high-context window analysis and creative generation.
    *   **Supabase (Embeddings):** Facilitated semantic search and similarity matching.
*   **Iterative Refinement:** Prompts were iteratively refined through "Dry-run Testing" (simulated execution) and "Semantic Versioning," ensuring that as the underlying models evolved, the prompt effectiveness remained high.
